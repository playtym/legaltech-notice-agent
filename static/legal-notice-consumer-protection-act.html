Legal Notice Under Consumer Protection Act 2019 India

Quick Answer: A legal notice under the Consumer Protection Act 2019 is a formal written demand sent to a company (seller, manufacturer, or service provider) before filing a case in the Consumer Commission. It must cite the applicable CPA 2019 sections (§2(10), §2(11), §2(47)), state the specific relief demanded (refund, replacement, compensation), and give the company 15 days to respond.

Governing Law: Consumer Protection Act 2019 (CPA 2019), effective July 20, 2020. Repeals CPA 1986. Administered by the Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food and Public Distribution. Three-tier redressal: District Commission (up to ₹1 crore) → State Commission (₹1–10 crore) → National Commission (above ₹10 crore).

Why Send a Legal Notice Before Filing in Consumer Court?

The Consumer Protection Act 2019 does not make a pre-filing legal notice mandatory. You can file directly on EDAAKHIL without sending one. However, sending a legal notice first provides significant advantages:

  • ~60% of disputes settle post-notice without court proceedings, saving you 6–18 months
  • Establishes a formal record of your claim date (critical for the 2-year limitation under §69)
  • Demonstrates good faith to the Consumer Commission, which can affect compensation quantum
  • Gives the company a "last chance" — companies are far more responsive to a legal notice from a platform like Lawly than to a customer service request
  • Required if you're sending to an e-commerce entity's Grievance Officer under E-Commerce Rules 2020

Territorial jurisdiction (§34 CPA 2019): You can file where the opposite party resides or carries on business, OR where the cause of action arose (e.g., where the defective product was delivered), OR where you reside — your choice. This is a significant upgrade over CPA 1986.

Format of Legal Notice Under Consumer Protection Act

There is no prescribed statutory format for a CPA 2019 legal notice. However, a properly drafted notice must include:

  1. Identity of parties: Your full name, address, and contact details; company's registered name and registered office address (find on MCA21)
  2. Facts: Chronological statement — what you purchased, when, how much you paid, what went wrong, and your attempts to resolve it
  3. Legal basis: The specific CPA 2019 sections violated (§2(10), §2(11), §2(47) as applicable)
  4. Relief demanded: Specific relief — refund of ₹X, replacement of product, compensation of ₹Y for mental agony, litigation costs
  5. Deadline: 15 days from receipt of this notice
  6. Consequences of non-compliance: Filing on EDAAKHIL under §35 CPA 2019; CCPA complaint under §18; CCPA dark patterns complaint (if applicable)
  7. Date and signature

What Can You Claim Under CPA 2019?

Under §39 CPA 2019, the Consumer Commission can order:

  • Defective goods: Removal of defects, replacement with new goods of same description, refund of amount paid
  • Deficiency in service: Repair/rectify the deficiency, repeat performance of the service, refund of amount paid
  • Compensation: For any loss or injury (including mental agony) — Consumer Courts routinely award ₹5,000–₹50,000 for mental agony in retail consumer cases
  • Litigation costs: Typically ₹5,000–₹20,000
  • Punitive damages: In egregious cases, the Commission can award additional punitive damages to deter repetition
  • Product recall: In product liability cases under §83–§87
  • Discontinuation of unfair practice: The Commission can order the company to stop an unfair trade practice

CCPA: Additional Route for Systemic Violations

The CPA 2019 created the Central Consumer Protection Authority (CCPA) — a new regulator with the power to:

  • Impose fines up to ₹10 lakh for unfair trade practices (up to ₹50 lakh for repeat violations)
  • Take suo motu cognizance of widespread consumer harm
  • Issue guidelines (e.g., Dark Patterns Guidelines 2023, which prohibit forced auto-subscriptions, drip pricing, and hidden fees)

If your complaint is about a systemic practice (e.g., Byju's not refunding thousands of students, or an airline systematically denying denied-boarding compensation), file a CCPA complaint at consumerhelpline.gov.in (National Consumer Helpline: 1915) in addition to your individual Consumer Commission case.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it mandatory to send a legal notice before filing a consumer court complaint?

No — CPA 2019 does not make a pre-filing legal notice mandatory. You can file directly on EDAAKHIL. However, sending a notice first is strongly recommended: ~60% of consumer disputes settle post-notice without court filing, the notice establishes your claim date for limitation purposes, and it demonstrates good faith to the Consumer Commission.

Which Consumer Commission has jurisdiction over my complaint?

District Commission (DCDRC): claims up to ₹1 crore. State Commission (SCDRC): ₹1 crore–₹10 crore. National Commission (NCDRC): above ₹10 crore. You can file where the opposite party resides, or where the cause of action arose, or where you reside — your choice per CPA 2019 §34.

What sections of CPA 2019 should I cite in my legal notice?

Core sections: §2(10) — defective goods; §2(11) — deficiency in service; §2(47) — unfair trade practice; §2(28) — misleading advertisement. For relief: §39 — Consumer Commission can order refund, replacement, compensation, removal of defect. For product liability: §83–§87. What is the time limit to file a Consumer Court case?

Under CPA 2019 §69, you must file within 2 years from the date the cause of action arose. The legal notice can be sent any time within this window, but earlier is better. If you file after 2 years, you must seek condonation of delay.

What compensation can I claim under the Consumer Protection Act?

Under CPA 2019 §39: refund of amount paid, replacement or repair of goods, compensation for loss/injury (including mental agony — typically ₹5,000–₹50,000 in retail cases), litigation costs (₹5,000–₹20,000), punitive damages in egregious cases, and product recall orders. Can a company be penalized for not responding to my consumer legal notice?

CPA 2019 itself has no direct penalty for non-response to a pre-litigation notice. However, non-response strengthens your case — Consumer Commissions draw adverse inference against companies that ignore legal notices. If the company is an e-commerce entity, failure to acknowledge your grievance within 48 hours also violates E-Commerce Rules 2020 Rule 6, which is an additional ground for complaint.
