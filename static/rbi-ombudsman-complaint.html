RBI Complaint Online — How to File via RBI Ombudsman Portal (2026)

The RBI Integrated Ombudsman is India's fastest and most effective channel for resolving banking, UPI, credit card, NBFC, and payment app disputes — completely free, online, and without a lawyer. Over 95% of complaints are resolved within 30–90 days.

RBI Ombudsman at a Glance

  • 🏛️ Portal: cms.rbi.org.in (24x7)
  • 📞 Helpline: 14448 (Mon–Fri, 9 AM–5 PM, toll-free)
  • ✅ Completely free — no filing fee, no lawyer needed
  • ⚖️ Binding orders — bank must comply or appeal to Appellate Authority
  • ⏱️ Resolution in 30–90 days typically
  • ⚠️ Prerequisite: Must complain to the bank first and wait 30 days

What Is the RBI Integrated Ombudsman?

The RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme (RBI-IOS), launched in November 2021, is a unified dispute resolution mechanism replacing the earlier three separate ombudsman schemes (Banking Ombudsman, NBFC Ombudsman, Digital Payments Ombudsman). It is administered by the Reserve Bank of India and covers all RBI-regulated entities under a single centralised portal — cms.rbi.org.in.

Unlike the Consumer Court (which takes 6–12 months), the RBI Ombudsman is faster, free, and has binding authority to order the bank to pay you back — including compensation for mental agony up to ₹1 lakh.

What Banking Complaints Are Covered?

🏦 Commercial & Co-operative Banks
  • Failed ATM withdrawals — cash not dispensed
  • Unauthorized debit/credit card transactions
  • UPI/NEFT/IMPS/RTGS failure — money deducted but not credited
  • Delayed or wrong credit of funds
  • Excessive bank charges without consent
  • Failure to close account or return documents
  • Mis-selling of insurance or investment products by bank staff
  • Non-adherence to KYC norms causing account freeze
  • Wrongful classification as NPA (Non-Performing Asset)
  • Credit card billing disputes
📱 Payment Service Providers
  • UPI app failures (PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm, GPay)
  • Prepaid wallet (PPI) balance issues
  • Failed BBPS (bill payment) transactions
  • Unauthorized wallet charges
🏢 NBFCs (Non-Banking Finance Companies)
  • Excessive interest rate charges
  • Unfair loan recovery practices / harassment
  • Non-return of property documents after loan closure
  • Forced insurance on loans (life insurance bundled with loans)
  • Loan account statement errors

Not covered: Stock market / SEBI complaints (go to scores.gov.in), Insurance (go to igms.irda.gov.in), General consumer e-commerce disputes (go to NCH 1915 or EDAAKHIL). The RBI Ombudsman only covers RBI-regulated entities.

Are You Eligible to File? — Checklist

You can file with the RBI Ombudsman if ALL of the following are true:

  • ✅ You have already filed a complaint directly with the bank/NBFC/payment provider
  • ✅ The bank either rejected your complaint, OR did not respond within 30 days
  • ✅ You are filing within 1 year of the bank's rejection or the 30-day non-response window
  • ✅ The complaint involves an RBI-regulated entity (bank, NBFC, UPI app, PPI issuer)
  • ✅ You have not already filed the same complaint in consumer court or arbitration

How to File an RBI Complaint Online — Step by Step

Step 1 — Prerequisite

File with Your Bank First (and Wait 30 Days)

The Ombudsman requires proof that you complained to the bank first. Save: (a) your original complaint with the bank (ticket/reference number), (b) the bank's response or proof they didn't respond in 30 days. Without this, your Ombudsman application will be rejected.

Step 2

Send a Legal Notice to the Bank (Recommended)

Before going to the Ombudsman, send the bank a formal legal notice via Lawly citing the relevant RBI circular. Many banks resolve at this stage to avoid regulatory scrutiny. This notice also becomes evidence of the bank's non-responsiveness.

Step 3

Visit cms.rbi.org.in

Go to cms.rbi.org.in. Click "File a Complaint". There is no account registration required — fill in your details as a guest. The portal is mobile-friendly and available 24x7.

Step 4

Select Complaint Category

Choose the type of regulated entity: Bank, NBFC, Payment Service Provider, or Credit Information Company. Then select the specific issue from the dropdown (e.g., "Failure to credit proceeds of payment instruments", "Unauthorized debit transactions", etc.).

Step 5

Fill in Complaint Details

Enter: your full name, mobile, email, bank name, account/card number, branch, a detailed description of the problem with the exact date and amounts, your prior complaint reference number and outcome, and the specific relief you seek (refund of ₹X + compensation of ₹Y).

Step 6

Upload Evidence

Attach as PDFs: bank statement showing the disputed transaction, your original complaint to the bank, the bank's rejection letter or proof of non-response, SMS/email confirmations, and any related documents (e.g., UPI app screenshots, ATM receipt).

Step 7

Submit and Track

Submit the form. You receive a registration number and confirmation by email and SMS. Track the status 24x7 at cms.rbi.org.in using your registration number. The Ombudsman's office will contact both you and the bank.

Documents to Upload with Your RBI Complaint

  • Bank account statement / credit card statement showing the disputed transaction
  • Screenshot or SMS of the failed/unauthorized transaction
  • Your original complaint to the bank (email, app chat, or written complaint)
  • Bank's response (rejection letter, or IT confirmation that no response came within 30 days)
  • National Consumer Helpline complaint if filed separately
  • Legal notice sent to the bank (if any)
  • For fraudulent transactions: FIR copy from police (strengthens your case)

What Happens After You File?

  1. Acknowledgement — Immediate auto-confirmation by email/SMS with a registration number.
  2. Bank notified — RBI Ombudsman's office forwards your complaint to the bank's nodal team. The bank must respond within 15-30 days.
  3. Mediation attempt — The Ombudsman's office attempts to facilitate a mutual settlement. Many cases resolve here.
  4. Award (if unresolved) — If mediation fails, the Ombudsman issues a binding Award directing the bank to: return the money + pay compensation for loss + pay compensation for mental agony (up to ₹1 lakh).
  5. Bank compliance — The bank must implement the Award within 30 days. If they disagree, they can appeal to the RBI Appellate Authority — but cannot ignore the order.

Appealing an Ombudsman Decision You Disagree With

If you are dissatisfied with the Ombudsman's Award or if the complaint is rejected, you can appeal to the Appellate Authority — the Executive Director or Deputy Governor in charge of the Banking Ombudsman at RBI — within 30 days of receiving the award. The appeal can be filed at cms.rbi.org.in itself using your complaint number.

Alternatively, you can still approach the Consumer Forum via EDAAKHIL for the same dispute — the Ombudsman process does not bar you from consumer court if you are unsatisfied.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the RBI Ombudsman toll-free number?▼
The RBI Integrated Ombudsman toll-free helpline is 14448, available Monday to Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM. For online filing anytime, use cms.rbi.org.in.
Can I file with the RBI Ombudsman for a failed UPI payment?▼
Yes. Failed UPI transactions are covered. RBI mandates auto-reversal within T+5 working days plus ₹100/day compensation for delay. File at cms.rbi.org.in under 'Payment Service Provider' (if the UPI app is at fault) or 'Bank' (if your bank account is affected).
What types of complaints does the RBI Ombudsman handle?▼
Banks (unauthorized transactions, ATM failures, loan mis-selling, credit card disputes), NBFCs (excessive interest, harassment, document non-return), Payment service providers (UPI, PPI wallets, BBPS), and Credit information companies (wrong CIBIL scores). It does NOT cover insurance (IRDAI) or stock markets (SEBI).
Do I need to hire a lawyer to file with the RBI Ombudsman?▼
No. The process is designed for consumers to self-represent. It is entirely online at cms.rbi.org.in, free of charge, and takes about 20 minutes to file. No advocate is needed or required.
What is the time limit for filing an RBI Ombudsman complaint?▼
File within 1 year of receiving the bank's rejection, or within 1 year of the 30-day window passing without a bank response. File as soon as possible — the sooner the better.
What compensation can I get from the RBI Ombudsman?▼
The Ombudsman can award: (1) Full recovery of the disputed amount (principal), (2) Compensation for mental agony up to ₹1 lakh, (3) Cost of complaint filing. For larger compensation (beyond ₹1 lakh in mental agony), you would need to additionally approach consumer court via EDAAKHIL.
