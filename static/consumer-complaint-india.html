Ways to File a Consumer Complaint in India

There are four main channels available to Indian consumers. Use them in this order — each escalates the pressure on the company:

NCH Helpline (1915) Informal mediation, quick resolution Free 15–30 days

Pro tip: About 60% of consumer complaints resolve after a properly drafted legal notice is sent — even before reaching the Consumer Forum. A legal notice signals you are serious and know your legal rights.

Step-by-Step: How to File a Consumer Complaint

Step 1

Document your grievance

Gather all evidence before doing anything else. This includes: order IDs and receipts, screenshots of the problem, all customer support chats and emails (with dates and ticket numbers), photos or videos of defective products, and any company communication. Write a clear one-paragraph summary of: what happened, which company is responsible, what financial loss you suffered, and what resolution you want.

Step 2

Escalate to the company's Grievance Officer

Under the Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules 2020, every platform must appoint a Grievance Officer. Standard customer support agents can only offer scripts — the Grievance Officer has authority to escalate. Send your complaint in writing (email or registered post), keep the ticket/reference number, and note the date. Allow 15 business days for a response. This creates a paper trail essential for later escalation.

Step 3

Send a formal legal notice

If the Grievance Officer fails to resolve the issue within 15–30 days, send a legal notice to the company's registered address. A legal notice must cite the relevant statute (Consumer Protection Act 2019, E-Commerce Rules 2020, RERA 2016, etc.), state the specific violation, quantify damages, and demand resolution within 15–30 days. Send via registered post — keep the postal receipt. Lawly generates professionally drafted legal notices in under 5 minutes starting at ₹199.

Step 4

File with the National Consumer Helpline (1915)

Call 1915 (toll-free, 24×7) or file online at consumerhelpline.gov.in. The NCH is run by the Ministry of Consumer Affairs and connects your complaint directly to the company's nodal officer. The NCH can conciliate disputes informally — no lawyers needed, no court fees. Run simultaneously with Step 3 for best results.

Step 5

File on EDAAKHIL — the Consumer Court online portal

If everything above fails, file a formal case on edaakhil.nic.in — the Government of India's online portal for Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission cases. You can file, pay fees, upload documents, and track hearings entirely online. The legal notice from Step 3 significantly strengthens your case. See our complete consumer court online complaint guide for a step-by-step EDAAKHIL walkthrough.

Step 6

Attend Consumer Forum hearings

You can self-represent under Section 35(1) of the Consumer Protection Act 2019 — no advocate required. The Commission issues summons to the company. Cases are adjudicated typically within 3–5 months. If the company does not comply with the Commission's order, it is punishable with imprisonment up to 3 years and fines up to ₹50,000 under Section 72 of CPA 2019.

National Consumer Helpline — 1915

The National Consumer Helpline (NCH) is India's primary first-response channel for consumer grievances. It is free, available 24×7, and supports 17 languages including Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Bengali, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, and Punjabi.

NCH Contact Details

  • Helpline Number: 1915 (toll-free, 24×7)
  • Old number (still works): 1800-11-4000 and 14404
  • Portal: consumerhelpline.gov.in
  • Email: nch@nic.in
  • Mobile App: "Consumer Helpline" on Android and iOS
  • WhatsApp: Available via NCH mobile app

What NCH can do

The NCH works with 1,000+ companies through a Convergence Partner network. When you call, NCH: logs your complaint, forwards it to the company's nodal officer, follows up, and provides you a docket number. For convergence partners (major e-commerce, banking, telecom companies), resolution typically happens within 15 days.

NCH cannot issue binding orders. If the company refuses to cooperate, you must escalate to EDAAKHIL.

EDAAKHIL — How to File Consumer Court Complaint Online

EDAAKHIL (edaakhil.nic.in) is the Government of India's e-filing portal for the Consumer Disputes Redressal Commissions. It replaced physical filing and allows cases to be filed, tracked, and paid for entirely online.

How to file on EDAAKHIL — step by step

  1. Register: Create an account at edaakhil.nic.in. You need a mobile number, email ID, and Aadhaar or PAN for verification.
  2. Select the correct Commission: District (up to ₹1 crore), State (₹1–10 crore), or National (above ₹10 crore).
  3. Select jurisdiction: You can file where the opposite party has a registered office/branch, or where the cause of action arose.
  4. Fill the complaint form: Complainant details, respondent's registered name and address, brief facts (chronological), relief sought.
  5. Attach documents: Receipts, screenshots, legal notice copy + registered post receipt, correspondence with company. Upload as PDF, max 5 MB each.
  6. Pay the filing fee: ₹100–₹5,000 through the online payment gateway (UPI, net banking, debit/credit card).
  7. Submit and track: You will receive a case number. Hearings are listed on EDAAKHIL. You can attend via video conferencing.

Why a Legal Notice Matters Before Filing in Court

A legal notice is a formal written demand sent to the company before initiating court proceedings. Under Indian practice, most consumer forums look favorably at cases where a prior legal notice was sent — it demonstrates good faith and that you attempted resolution.

Why it works: Companies receive thousands of customer service tickets. A legal notice — especially one that correctly cites CPA 2019, calculates compensation, and names the exact legal provisions violated — signals that you are serious and legally informed. Most companies have legal departments that classify legal notices separately from support tickets and prioritize resolution.

Statistics: According to consumer advocates, approximately 60% of legal notice cases are settled within 30 days without any court involvement. This saves months of effort and Forum fees.

Which Consumer Forum Should You Approach?

The Consumer Protection Act 2019 sets up a three-tier commission structure:

  • District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: Claims up to ₹1 crore. There is one in every district. File where the opposite party operates or where the cause of action arose.
  • State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission: Claims between ₹1 crore and ₹10 crore. Also handles appeals from District Commissions.
  • National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (NCDRC): Claims above ₹10 crore. Handles appeals from State Commissions. Located in New Delhi.

For most everyday disputes (e-commerce, banking, telecom, insurance), the District Commission is the correct forum. Use Lawly's jurisdiction checker or consult your local consumer court guide below.

Sector-Specific Complaint Channels

For certain sectors, you should simultaneously (or instead) use the dedicated regulator's complaint channel:

Banking & Finance

  • RBI Integrated Ombudsman: cms.rbi.org.in — covers all bank disputes including UPI, credit cards, ATM fraud, unauthorized debit. Free, no advocate needed, resolved in 30 days typically.
  • Covers: UPI payment failures, failed ATM transactions, unauthorized charges, credit card disputes, home loan overcharging.

Insurance

  • IRDAI IGMS Portal: igms.irda.gov.in — for insurance claim rejections, policy disputes, premium issues.
  • Insurance Ombudsman: 17 offices across India. Free, binding. Covers life, health, and general insurance disputes up to ₹30 lakh.

Telecom

  • TRAI DND: dnd.trai.gov.in — for spam calls, Do Not Disturb violations.
  • DoT CPGRAMS: pgportal.gov.in — for service quality complaints against Jio, Airtel, Vi, BSNL.

Real Estate (RERA)

  • Each state has its own RERA authority. File complaints for builder delays, possession issues, and quality defects directly with the State RERA portal. Separate from Consumer Forum — you can use both simultaneously.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I file a consumer complaint online in India for free?
Use two official free channels: (1) National Consumer Helpline at consumerhelpline.gov.in (or call 1915) for informal mediation; (2) EDAAKHIL at edaakhil.nic.in for formal Consumer Forum filing with fees as low as ₹100 for claims up to ₹5 lakh.
What is the National Consumer Helpline number?
The National Consumer Helpline is 1915 (toll-free, 24×7, available in 17 languages). Old numbers 1800-11-4000 and 14404 still work. You can also email nch@nic.in or file at consumerhelpline.gov.in.
What is EDAAKHIL and how do I use it?
EDAAKHIL (edaakhil.nic.in) is the Government of India's online portal for filing Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission cases. Register, select your commission, fill in complaint details, upload documents, and pay the filing fee online. You receive a case number and can track hearings from home.
Do I need a lawyer to file a consumer complaint?
No. Section 35(1) of the Consumer Protection Act 2019 explicitly allows self-representation at all Consumer Commissions. Before approaching the forum, however, send a legal notice. Lawly generates these at ₹199 and about 60% of cases settle at this stage alone.
Which consumer forum should I approach?
District Commission for claims up to ₹1 crore; State Commission for ₹1–10 crore; National Commission above ₹10 crore. File in the district where the company operates or where your cause of action arose. Most consumer complaints fall under District Commission jurisdiction.
What is the consumer complaint email ID?
National Consumer Helpline: nch@nic.in. Sector-specific: RBI Ombudsman via cms.rbi.org.in; IRDAI via igms.irda.gov.in; TRAI via dnd.trai.gov.in; DGCA via airsewa.gov.in.
How long does it take to resolve a consumer complaint?
NCH mediation: 15–30 days. Legal notice response: 15–30 days. Consumer Forum order: 3–5 months. Sector regulators (RBI, IRDAI): 30–90 days. Complex cases or appeals can take longer.
Can I file a consumer complaint against an e-commerce company?
Yes. E-commerce platforms are covered under the Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules 2020 and the Consumer Protection Act 2019. They must have a Grievance Officer and resolve complaints within 15 days. If they fail, send a legal notice via Lawly and file on EDAAKHIL.
