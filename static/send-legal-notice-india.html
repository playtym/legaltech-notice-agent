Why a Legal Notice Works

Most large Indian companies — Amazon, Flipkart, Zomato, banks, insurance companies — have a legal or compliance desk that monitors incoming legal notices. A properly formatted notice citing applicable law sections creates immediate internal escalation that a customer service complaint does not.

  • Escalation trigger: A notice with statute citations goes straight to the company's legal team, bypassing regular customer service
  • Settlement pressure: Companies prefer settling for ₹5,000–₹50,000 over spending ₹50,000+ defending a Consumer Forum case
  • Limitation on defences: If you later go to the Consumer Forum, an unanswered notice demonstrates the company's bad faith
  • Proof of prior attempt: Consumer commissions view a prior notice favourably when awarding compensation for mental agony and litigation costs

Did you know? Under Section 69 of CPA 2019, you have 2 years from when the cause of action arose to file a consumer complaint. Do not wait — send your legal notice as soon as the company fails to resolve your complaint after the first escalation attempt.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can I generate and send a legal notice with Lawly?
The AI generates your notice in under 5 minutes. You can print and send via Registered Post the same day. Total time: under 1 hour from complaint to notice in the post.

Is an AI-generated legal notice legally valid in India?
Yes. Indian law does not require a notice to be drafted by an advocate. Lawly's AI ensures correct statute citations, proper legal language, and correct formatting — equivalent to a lawyer-drafted notice.

What if the company ignores my legal notice?
After the response deadline (15–30 days), file a formal complaint on EDAAKHIL. Attach the notice and postal delivery proof. See our EDAAKHIL filing guide.

Can I send the notice to multiple addresses?
Yes — send to both the registered office and the Grievance Officer (mandatory for e-commerce companies). Sending by email simultaneously is also recommended for quick commerce and e-commerce companies.
