RERA Complaint Online — How to File Against Your Builder (2026)

The Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA) gives homebuyers legally enforceable rights against builders for delay, refund, possession, and quality defects. RERA complaints resolve in 60–90 days — far faster than civil courts. This guide covers the complete process with state-wise portal links.

RERA at a Glance

  • ⚖️ Law: Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016
  • 🏛️ Forum: State RERA Authority (each state has its own portal)
  • ⏱️ Decision in: 60 days (extendable to 90)
  • 💰 You can claim: Interest for delay + full refund + compensation
  • 📋 Applicable: Projects with >8 units or >500 sq m area
  • 💸 Filing fee: ₹1,000–₹5,000 depending on state

Your Rights Under RERA — What Can You Claim?

RightRERA SectionWhat You Get
Delayed possessionSection 18Interest at SBI MCLR + 2% per annum for every month of delay. You can stay in the project OR exit and get full refund + this interest.
Full refund (exit)Section 18(1)All amounts paid + interest from the date of payment, if you choose to exit due to builder's default.
False informationSection 12Compensation for any loss suffered due to false/incomplete information in brochure, sale agreement, or advertising.
Structural defectsSection 14(3)Builder must fix any structural defect reported within 5 years of possession, at their own cost, within 30 days.
Non-registrationSection 3If the builder sold/marketed the project without RERA registration, they are liable for penalty up to 10% of project cost.
Title defectSection 18(3)Compensation for any loss due to defective title of land sold by the builder — no time limit for this claim.

Step 1 — Send a Legal Notice to Your Builder First

Before filing on the RERA portal, send a formal legal notice to your builder. This is not mandatory under RERA but is strongly recommended because:

  1. Many builders settle immediately when they receive a legal notice, to avoid RERA scrutiny and penalties
  2. It creates a dated paper trail proving when you raised the issue
  3. It becomes key evidence in your RERA case
  4. It demonstrates good faith to the RERA authority

Your legal notice to the builder should cite: the original possession date in your agreement, the actual delay, Section 18 of the RERA Act (for delayed possession or refund), and a specific demand with a 15-day response deadline.

How to File a RERA Complaint Online — Step by Step

Step 1

Send Legal Notice to Builder (Recommended First)

Use Lawly to send a formal legal notice citing Section 18 RERA. Many builders settle within 15 days. If not, proceed to the RERA portal.

Step 2

Verify Project RERA Registration

Before filing, verify the project is registered under RERA on your state's portal. Search by project name or builder name. Note the RERA registration number — mandatory for the complaint form.

Step 3

Register on Your State RERA Portal

Go to the relevant state portal (see list above). Create an account with your mobile number and email OTP verification. Most state portals have a dedicated "File Complaint" or "Grievances" section.

Step 4

Fill the Complaint Form

Enter: your full name, mobile, email, PAN, address; builder/promoter name, project RERA number, project name and address; nature of complaint (delay / refund / quality defect / false information); original possession date from agreement, actual status, amount paid to date; and specific relief sought (e.g. "interest payment of ₹X under Section 18 for Y months of delay").

Step 5

Upload Documents and Pay Filing Fee

Upload all documents (see list below). Then pay the complaint filing fee online — ₹1,000–₹5,000 depending on the state. Keep the payment receipt — you'll need it for tracking.

Step 6

Attend Hearings & Track Status

RERA serves notice on your builder within 15 days. Hearings are typically online or in the RERA office. You may present your case yourself or send an advocate. RERA must decide within 60 days of filing.

Documents Required for RERA Complaint

  • Builder-Buyer Agreement / Sale Agreement (registered)
  • Allotment letter from the builder
  • All payment receipts / demand letters paid
  • RERA registration certificate of the project (from state RERA portal)
  • Builder's possession letter (if given — even if possession not actually given)
  • Legal notice sent to builder (if sent)
  • Builder's response to legal notice (if any)
  • Original brochure / advertisement that made specific promises
  • Any communications with the builder regarding delay/refund
  • Bank statements showing payments made
  • ID proof (Aadhaar/PAN)

What Happens After You File a RERA Complaint?

  1. Acknowledgement — Instant acknowledgement with complaint registration number.
  2. Notice to builder — RERA serves notice to your builder within 15 days, asking them to respond within 30 days.
  3. Builder's reply — Builder files their written response. You get a copy and can file a rejoinder.
  4. Hearing — RERA schedules a hearing (online or in-person). Both sides present documents and arguments. You can represent yourself without a lawyer.
  5. RERA Order — Within 60 days, RERA issues a written order. If in your favour, the builder must comply within 45 days — including paying the ordered amount.
  6. Non-compliance — If the builder ignores the RERA order, RERA can impose additional penalties (up to 10% of project cost) and even prosecute the promoter for up to 3 years imprisonment.
  7. Appeal — Either party may appeal RERA's order to the RERA Appellate Tribunal within 60 days.

Important: If your builder files for insolvency (IBC proceedings), your claim as a homebuyer has Financial Creditor status under IBC amendments — you are prioritised over operational creditors. File your claim with the Resolution Professional immediately if your builder has entered insolvency proceedings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I file a RERA complaint online?▼
Yes. All major state RERA authorities have online portals. MahaRERA (Maharashtra), UP RERA, Karnataka RERA, DRERA (Delhi), Haryana RERA, Gujarat RERA, and others all allow end-to-end online filing, document upload, and video hearings.
What can I claim in a RERA complaint?▼
Under Section 18: interest at SBI MCLR + 2% per annum for every month of delay, or full refund of all amounts + interest if you choose to exit. Under Section 14(3): structural defect rectification within 5 years free of cost. Under Section 12: compensation for losses from false project information. You can usually claim multiple remedies.
What is the RERA complaint fee in Maharashtra?▼
MahaRERA charges ₹5,000 per complaint, paid online on the maharera.mahaonline.gov.in portal. This is the highest filing fee among all states. Other states generally charge ₹1,000.
Is legal notice to builder mandatory before RERA?▼
Not legally required, but strongly recommended. It often results in a quick settlement, creates a dated paper trail, and becomes important evidence in your RERA case. Many builders resolve delays or refund disputes immediately after receiving a formal legal notice rather than face RERA proceedings.
How long does RERA take to resolve a complaint?▼
RERA is mandated to resolve complaints within 60 days (extendable to 90 days with recorded reasons). In practice, contested cases may take 3–6 months for a final order. This is still dramatically faster than civil courts (5–10 years) or National Consumer Forum complex cases (1–2 years).
Can I file RERA if the builder is not registered?▼
Yes. Non-registration itself is a RERA violation. File the complaint with RERA about the non-registration — RERA can take suo motu action against the builder, impose penalties (up to 10% of project cost), and even prosecute the promoter. You can simultaneously approach the consumer court or send a legal notice for the underlying dispute.
