Refund Not Received? Here's How to Force Any Company to Pay (2026)

Whether Amazon says "refund initiated" or Flipkart keeps delaying, you have legal rights under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019. A formal legal notice resolves most refund disputes within 7 days — without going to court. This guide covers every escalation step.

Your Rights at a Glance

  • ⚖️ Legal basis: Consumer Protection Act, 2019 + E-Commerce Rules 2020
  • 💬 Grievance Officer: Must respond within 48 hours, resolve in 1 month
  • 📞 Consumer Helpline: 1915 (free, 24x7)
  • 💰 You can claim: Full refund + compensation for harassment + legal costs
  • ⚡ Legal notice: Works in 7 days for 80% of cases
  • 🏛️ Consumer court: If nothing else works — order in 3–6 months

Step-by-Step: How to Force a Refund

Step 1 — Customer Support

Re-escalate with Documentation

Call or chat with customer support. Give: your order ID, the original complaint reference number, the exact refund amount, and the date refund was promised. Ask: "Please escalate to your Grievance Officer" and "Give me confirmation in writing." Screenshot or save the chat/email.

Step 2 — Grievance Officer

Email the Company's Grievance Officer

Under Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020, every registered e-commerce company must appoint a Grievance Officer and publish their contact on the website. Find the email under "Legal", "About", or "Help Centre" pages. Send a formal email with all details. Law requires: response within 48 hours, resolution within 1 month.

Step 3 — Legal Notice (Most Effective)

Send a Formal Legal Notice via Lawly

A legal notice citing the Consumer Protection Act, 2019 and demanding refund + compensation within 15 days is the single most effective lever. Most companies (even Amazon and Flipkart) have legal teams that process refunds immediately upon receiving a legal notice to avoid consumer court exposure. Generate your legal notice at Lawly →

Step 4 — NCH 1915

File with National Consumer Helpline

Call 1915 (toll-free, 24x7) or file online at consumerhelpline.gov.in. Major platforms (Amazon, Flipkart, Zomato, Swiggy, MakeMyTrip, etc.) are part of the INGRAM network, meaning NCH complaints are automatically escalated. Resolution typically in 15–30 days.

Step 5 — Consumer Court

File Complaint at EDAAKHIL

File at edaakhil.nic.in under the Consumer Protection Act. For amounts up to ₹50 lakh, file in your District Consumer Forum. Fee: ₹100–₹500. You can claim refund + compensation for harassment + cost of legal notice. Most companies settle before the first hearing date to avoid adverse orders. Full guide to EDAAKHIL filing →

What to Demand — Beyond Just the Refund

Under Consumer Protection Act, 2019, you are entitled to more than just the refund amount. Your legal notice and/or consumer court complaint should demand:

  • Full refund of the original amount paid
  • Interest on delayed refund (typically 12–18% per annum from the date refund was due)
  • Compensation for mental agony and harassment — courts have awarded ₹5,000–₹50,000 depending on the distress caused and the time wasted
  • Cost of legal notice (Lawly's ₹199–₹599 fee)
  • Cost of litigation (if filing in consumer court)

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the law give a company to process a refund?▼
Under Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules 2020, companies must process refunds within the timeframe stated in their published refund policy. Most platforms promise 5–7 business days. If not received by then, the delay constitutes 'deficiency in service' under the Consumer Protection Act and you can claim compensation in addition to the refund.
What if Amazon/Flipkart shows 'refund initiated' but money not received?▼
Ask the company for the UTR/Transaction Reference Number (TRN) of the refund they claim to have initiated. With that number, your bank can trace the incoming credit. If the company cannot provide a valid TRN, the refund has not actually been processed — escalate via legal notice immediately.
Can I go to consumer court for a refund not received?▼
Yes. A delayed or denied refund is a recognized 'deficiency in service' under Customer Protection Act 2019. File in your District Consumer Forum (DCDRC) for amounts up to ₹50 lakh at edaakhil.nic.in. Court fee is just ₹100–₹500. Consumer courts routinely award the refund + compensation (₹5,000–₹50,000+) + legal costs. Most companies settle before the first hearing.
Does a legal notice really work for getting refunds?▼
In most cases, yes. A formal legal notice triggers escalation to the company's legal team, who process the refund to avoid the cost and reputational risk of consumer court proceedings. Companies like Amazon and Flipkart have dedicated legal response teams that typically process refunds within 3–7 days of a legal notice. A ₹199 Lawly legal notice often resolves disputes that months of customer care calls could not.
What is the Consumer Helpline number for refund complaints?▼
The National Consumer Helpline number is 1915 (toll-free, 24x7). You can file online at consumerhelpline.gov.in. Major e-commerce platforms are on their INGRAM network and must respond to NCH-mediated complaints.
