How to File a Case in Consumer Court Delhi

If you are facing an issue with a product, service, or builder in Delhi, you have the right to approach the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission in Delhi.

Step 1: Statutory Legal Notice (Mandatory)

Before judges in Delhi will hear your case, you must show proof that you sent a formal Legal Notice giving the company 15-30 days to resolve the issue.

Finding the Right Court in Delhi

Cases under ₹50 Lakhs are filed at the District level. In Delhi, ensure you select the correct jurisdiction (e.g., Delhi Urban, Delhi Rural) based on where the transaction occurred or where the opposite party resides.

E-Daakhil Portal

Citizens of Delhi can now file consumer complaints online via the E-Daakhil government portal. You will need to upload your ID, payment receipts, and the Legal Notice sent to the company.
