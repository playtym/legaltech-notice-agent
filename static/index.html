Frequently Asked Questions

Is this legally binding in Indian courts?
Yes. Under Indian law (e.g. CPA 2019), any individual can draft and send a legal notice on their own behalf perfectly legally. A well-drafted notice acts as a formal record of your grievance and is a standard pre-requisite before approaching a Consumer Forum or filing a civil suit.
What is the difference between Self-Send and Lawyer-Assisted?
With the Self-Send tier, you receive a meticulously drafted PDF immediately which you must print and post (or email) yourself. The Lawyer-Assisted tier involves a registered Advocate who reviews the draft, places it on their official letterhead, and serves it directly to the respondent using Registered Post Acknowledgement Due (RPAD), adding significant legal weight.
How long before I get a response?
Lawly notices impose a statutory cure period (typically 15 to 30 days, depending on the statute). We strongly recommend waiting for this cure period to expire. If the company fails to respond, you have established a solid "cause of action" and you may proceed to file your complaint with the e-Daakhil consumer forum or relevant ombudsman.
Is my data secure?
Yes. We do not sell or share your complaint details with third-party advertisers. All documents and data are encrypted and strictly kept confidential under attorney-client privilege equivalents in our Lawyer-Assisted workflows.
