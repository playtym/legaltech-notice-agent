What is E-Stamp Paper?

E-stamp paper is the digital replacement for traditional physical stamp paper in India. Issued by SHCIL (Stock Holding Corporation of India Ltd) — the Central Record Keeping Agency appointed by the Government of India — or by state-authorised vendors. Each certificate carries a Unique Identification Number (UIN) verifiable online, making it tamper-proof and court-admissible.

  • Legal basis: Indian Stamp Act 1899 and applicable state stamp acts
  • Available denominations: ₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100, ₹200, ₹500, ₹1,000, ₹5,000, ₹10,000, ₹25,000, ₹50,000
  • Documents requiring e-stamping: Rent/lease agreements, affidavits, power of attorney, NDAs, sale deeds, gift deeds, indemnity bonds, partnership deeds
  • Validity: 6 months from date of issuance — document must be signed within this window
  • Adoption: All 28 states and 8 Union Territories have adopted e-stamping
