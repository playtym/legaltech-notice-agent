What is E-Stamp Paper?

E-stamp paper (electronic stamp paper) is the digital replacement for traditional physical stamp paper in India. It is issued by SHCIL (Stock Holding Corporation of India Ltd) — the Central Record Keeping Agency (CRA) appointed by the Government of India — or by state-authorised vendors.

Each e-stamp certificate carries a Unique Identification Number (UIN) that can be verified online, making it tamper-proof. Documents executed on e-stamp paper are legally valid across India under the Indian Stamp Act 1899 and relevant state stamp acts.

  • Available denominations: ₹10, ₹20, ₹50, ₹100, ₹200, ₹500, ₹1,000, ₹5,000, ₹10,000, ₹15,000, ₹20,000, ₹25,000, ₹50,000
  • Who issues: SHCIL, state treasury offices, authorised bank branches, CSC centres, and licensed online platforms
  • Documents requiring e-stamping: Rent / lease agreements, affidavits, power of attorney, NDAs, sale deeds, gift deeds, indemnity bonds, partnership deeds
  • Registration: Some documents (sale deeds, long-term leases) also need registration at the Sub-Registrar's Office in addition to stamping

How to Use E-Stamp Paper — Step by Step

  1. Draft your document using the generator above. Download the PDF.
  2. Calculate stamp duty using the calculator above for your state and document type.
  3. Purchase e-stamp paper of the required denomination from SHCIL, your state portal, or an authorised vendor (LegalDesk, Legistify, etc.).
  4. Type/print the document on the e-stamp paper, or attach the e-stamp certificate as the first page (method varies by state).
  5. Sign and witness the document — both parties must sign, and ideally two witnesses should attest.
  6. Register the document at the Sub-Registrar's Office if required by law (mandatory for sale deeds, leases over 11 months, etc.).
  7. For affidavits: get it notarised by an Executive Magistrate or Notary Public after printing on stamp paper.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is an 11-month rent agreement valid without registration?

Yes. Under Section 17 of the Registration Act 1908, only leases exceeding 12 months need compulsory registration. An 11-month rent agreement on stamp paper of the correct value is legally valid and admissible as evidence in court, even without Sub-Registrar registration. However, it is advisable to register it for added protection.

Can I use a photocopy of e-stamp paper for a document?

No. Each e-stamp certificate has a unique UIN and can only be used once. You cannot photocopy or reuse an e-stamp certificate. Each new document needs a fresh e-stamp paper purchase.

How long does an e-stamp paper remain valid?

An e-stamp certificate is valid for 6 months from the date of issuance (as per SHCIL rules). The document must be executed (signed) within this period. After 6 months, the e-stamp expires and a fresh one must be purchased.

What happens if I use insufficient stamp duty?

An under-stamped (deficiently stamped) document cannot be used as evidence in court proceedings unless the deficient duty + penalty is paid. The penalty for deficient stamp duty is typically 2–10 times the deficient amount, depending on the state. Stamp duty officers can impound under-stamped documents.

Do I need to be physically present to get e-stamp paper?

No. Through online platforms like LegalDesk, Leegality, Legistify, or SHCIL's own portal, you can purchase e-stamp paper and have the printed document delivered to your address, or receive a digital e-stamp certificate. Some states also support Aadhaar-based digital execution (DigiLocker + e-sign) without any physical presence.
