What Is a Legal Notice?

A legal notice is a formal written communication sent to a person or organisation before initiating legal proceedings. It puts the other party on formal notice of your grievance and your intent to pursue legal remedies if the matter is not resolved. Under Indian consumer law, it demonstrates that you attempted resolution before approaching the Consumer Commission.

While not mandatory under the Consumer Protection Act 2019, sending a legal notice is strategically essential: approximately 60% of disputes settle after a properly worded notice reaches the company's legal team, saving months of Forum proceedings.

7 Mandatory Components of a Legal Notice

A legally effective notice must contain all these elements. Missing any one weakens your position significantly.

# Component What to Include
1 Sender Details Required Full legal name, complete address, phone number, email. If sending through a lawyer, mention "Through Advocate [Name]"
2 Recipient Details Required Company's full registered legal name (not brand name), registered office address (from MCA portal or their Terms page), and if applicable, the Grievance Officer's name and address
3 Date Required Date of drafting/sending
4 Statement of Facts Required Chronological timeline: date of purchase, nature of product/service, what went wrong, when you complained, how the company responded (or failed to). Reference all order IDs, ticket numbers, dates
5 Legal Provisions Violated Required Specific sections of applicable law — e.g., §2(11) definition of deficiency, §2(47) unfair trade practice, Chapter VI product liability (CPA 2019). This is where most DIY notices fail
6 Relief Demanded Required State exactly what you want: "Refund of ₹4,999 + ₹10,000 compensation for mental agony within 15 days." Be specific. Vague demands invite ignored notices.
7 Response Deadline Required Typically 15 or 30 days from receipt of notice. State that failure to respond will result in legal proceedings before the appropriate Consumer Commission without further notice.

Step-by-Step: How to Send a Legal Notice in India

1

Find the correct Opposite Party details

You need the company's registered legal name and registered office address — not just the brand. For example, Zomato's legal name is "Zomato Limited." Find it on:
• MCA portal: mca.gov.in
• The company's website — Terms of Service, Refund Policy, or "About Us" page (usually has legal entity name and registered office)
Also note the Grievance Officer's name and email if one is mandated (required for e-commerce companies under E-Commerce Rules 2020)

2

Collect all evidence

Gather every document that supports your case:
• Invoice / tax receipt / order confirmation
• Screenshots of the product listing, claims made, delivery status
• Photos of defective product, wrong item, expired packaging
• All email/chat correspondence with customer support
• Prior complaint reference numbers (NCH, company portal, email tickets)
• Bank statements showing payment / failed refund

3

Draft the legal notice

Write the notice using the 7 components above. Use formal English. Refer to yourself as "the Noticee" or "the undersigned." Refer to the company as "the Opposite Party" or "the Noticee." State the applicable law explicitly.

4

Print and sign the notice

Print the notice on plain A4. Sign at the bottom. If your notice is drafted through an advocate, the advocate signs it. Keep one signed photocopy for your records before sending.

5

Send via Registered Post with Acknowledgement Due (RPAD)

Go to any India Post branch with the sealed envelope addressed to the company's registered office.
• Ask for "Registered Post AD" (Acknowledgement Due / RPAD)
• Cost: approximately ₹50–₹100 depending on weight
• Postal clerk will give you a tracking receipt number — keep this safe
• The recipient's representative signs a red acknowledgement card, which India Post returns to you
• Both the receipt and the returned red card are proof of delivery admissible in Consumer Court

You can also send the notice by courier (keep the POD — proof of delivery) or email (with read receipt), but RPAD is the gold standard for legal proceedings.

6

Wait for the response deadline

The company has the number of days you stated in the notice (typically 15 or 30 days) to respond. Three outcomes:
• Company settles: Get the resolution in writing. Confirm the agreed amount/action before closing.
• Company responds but disputes: Evaluate their offer. If inadequate, proceed to Consumer Forum.
• No response: Proceed to EDAAKHIL filing. Attach the legal notice copy and the postal receipt/acknowledgement card as exhibits.

Sending a Legal Notice Without a Lawyer

You do not need an advocate to draft or send a legal notice in India. There is no provision in Indian law requiring a notice to bear an advocate's signature for consumer matters. A well-drafted notice from a consumer themselves carries full legal weight.

After Sending: What to Expect

  • Day 1–3: Company's legal/compliance team receives and logs the notice
  • Day 5–15: Many companies initiate internal review and contact the consumer proactively
  • Day 15–30: Majority of settlements happen in this window if the company intends to settle
  • After deadline: File a case on EDAAKHIL — attach the notice, postal receipt, and acknowledgement card

Which Consumer Forum? District Commission up to ₹1 crore. State Commission ₹1–10 crore. National Commission above ₹10 crore. File in the jurisdiction where the company's branch is located or where the transaction occurred. See our full EDAAKHIL filing guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it mandatory to send a legal notice before filing a consumer complaint?
No — but it is strongly recommended. About 60% of disputes settle post-notice. If you proceed to the Consumer Forum, a prior legal notice demonstrates good faith and strengthens your complaint.

Can I send a legal notice without a lawyer?
Yes. No Indian law requires an advocate's signature on a consumer legal notice. Lawly generates legally accurate notices for ₹199 without requiring a lawyer.

How do I send a legal notice by registered post?
Visit any India Post branch and request "Registered Post with Acknowledgement Due" (RPAD). Cost is ₹50–₹100. Keep your tracking receipt and the returned red acknowledgement card — these prove delivery in court.

What if the company doesn't reply to my legal notice?
Non-response doesn't help the company. After the deadline, file on EDAAKHIL and include the unanswered notice as Exhibit A. Consumer forums treat non-response as indicative of the deficiency.

How much does it cost to send a legal notice in India?

Can I send a legal notice by email or courier?
Yes, but Registered Post AD is preferred for court purposes. For e-commerce companies, sending simultaneously by email (to the Grievance Officer) and RPAD strengthens your position — the email proves immediate notice while RPAD proves formal delivery.
